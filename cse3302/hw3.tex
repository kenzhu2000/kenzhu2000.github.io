\section*{Problem 1 - 30\%}

Evaluate the following $\lambda$ expressions using call-by-value and call-by-name operational semantics ($e \rightarrow e'$) with \textbf{just one step}. 
(Hint: you may or may not be able to take a step on the given expression.)
Show the complete derivation tree of this one step for all three experessions 
if you can take step.
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
    \item $(\lambda v.\lambda w.w)\ ((\lambda x.x)\ (y\ \lambda z.z))$
    \item $(\lambda x.\ x\ x)\ (\lambda y.\ y\ y)$
    \item $(\lambda x. \lambda y. x)\ ((\lambda z.z)\ (\lambda u.u))$
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Problem 3 - 30\%}

Recall the definition of natural numbers by $n\ \mbox{nat}$ judgment taught in the lecture.
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
    \item Give an inductive definition of the judgment form
    $\mbox{fib}\ n_1\ n_2$, which indicates the $n_1^{th}$ Fibonacci number is $n_2$.
    \item Give an inductive definition of the judgment form
    $\mbox{fibsum}\ n_1\ n_2$, which indicates the sum of the first $n_1$ Fibonacci
    numbers is $n_2$.
    \item Prove by induction:
    If $\mbox{fibsum}\ n\ m$ then $\mbox{fib}\
    \mbox{succ}(\mbox{succ}(n))\ \mbox{succ}(m)$, that is
    $$\sum_{i=1}^n F_i = F_{n+2} - 1.$$
\end{enumerate}
